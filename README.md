##Business Problem##
A small business wants to track revenue, expenses, and profitability to understand financial performance and identify the most profitable months.

business sells products and services
management wants monthly financial visibility
goal is profitability monitoring

Data Set Includes

Month	
Product || Sales	Revenue from product sold
Service Revenue ||	Revenue from service provided
Other Income ||	Other Revenues
Total Revenue ||	Revenue from all 3
COGS ||	Cost of selling goods
Gross Profit || total revenue - COGS	
Marketing	Cost || cost spend on marketing
Salaries ||	Salaries Given to employees
Rent ||	Rent of workspace
Software/Tools ||	Cost of using software and tools
Other Expenses ||	Miscllaneous expenses
Total Operating Expenses || Marketing cost + Salaries + Rent + Software/Tools + Other Expenses	
Net Profit	|| Gross Profit - Total Operating Cost
Revenue Growth%	 || Revenue Growth %age month on Month
Net Profit Growth%	|| Net profit growth Month On Month

## P&L Structure 

Revenue = Product Sales + Service Revenue + Other Income
Gross Profit = Revenue − COGS
Net Profit = Gross Profit − Operating Expenses

##Key Metrics

Revenue → total income generated
Gross Profit → revenue after production costs
Net Profit → final business profitability
Profit Margin → percentage profit from revenue
Gross Margin → operational efficiency

#KPI Chart Explanation

Revenue vs Expense Trend  :-  Tracks revenue growth compared with operating expenses.
Revenue Breakdown :- Shows revenue contribution from product sales, services, and other income.
Expense Breakdown :- Highlights major cost drivers.

## Insights

Revenue peaked in November ($48,738).
Salaries represent 52% of total expenses, indicating labor-heavy operations.
The business maintains a 23% net profit margin, indicating healthy profitability.

## Recommendations

Monitor salary growth since it is the largest cost component.
Increase service revenue to diversify income sources.
Control marketing expenses during lower revenue months.
